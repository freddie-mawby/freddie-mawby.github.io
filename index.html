About Me

Hello! My name is Freddie, I am 20 years old and currently an undergraduate student of the double degree "Politics of the Earth" offered by Sciences Po Paris and the Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris. I am majoring in Political Humanities (political philosophy, history, and anthropology) and Earth Sciences (mathematics, physics, and chemistry applied to geosciences). During my exchange at University College Utrecht (Netherlands), I took a course in Geographic Information Systems and Sciences because I am interested in natural disaster management, and GIS plays an important part in that. This website is a compilation of all the maps I made for that course. All in one spot for your easy access ;)

Maps


This page features the maps I've made for my GIS course.
SDG Indicator 15.3.1 Choropleth Map (static)

This is a map of sustainable development goal indicator 15.3.1, which shows the percentage of degraded land for each country.
Made in QGIS, using data from the UNCCD.

Learn more
SDG Indicator 15.3.1 Choropleth Map (again, but interactive!)

Using the shapefile I got from the previous map, I an interactive version in Esri's Web App Builder. Click on any country to see its percentage of degraded land or filter the countries to see which ones are above or below a certain threshold of degradation.

Learn more
Snow Coverage Arve Valley (October 2021-March 2022)

This one was made using Google Earth Engine, and it highlights the snow coverage in that area using false color composite. The different layers show monthly changes between October 2021 and March 2022.
All data is from Sentinel 2 satellite.
